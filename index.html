Zoos, Ethics, and The Evolution of Captivity

By: Huzaifa Anis
A philosophical case study exploring animal captivity, conservation, and moral responsibilities throughout history.

Project Overview

This website will serve as an interactive case study exploring how zoos have evolved throughout history, displaying a timeline dating from early civilization to modern institutions. We will highlight historic events, conservation missions, and ethical concerns regarding the Captivity and treatment of animals.

Have modern zoos transformed into ethical conservation spaces for wildlife, or do their historical pattern indicate ethical concerns that remain unresolved in our society today?

Philosophical Framework

Peter Singer — All Animals Are Equal

  • All "self" beings have emotions, therefore, they deserve equal moral consideration.
  • He argues that treating humans as more important than other beings just because of our species reveals “speciesism.”
  • He emphasizes that zoos are justified only if they reduce the animals suffering

Tom Regan — The Case for Animal Rights

  • Regan explains that animals also have rights, especially the right not to be treated as objects or property.
  • Regan reveals that captivity is the main issue

Pepper — Privacy Rights & Animal Agency

  • Pepper argues that many animals also care about privacy and having control over their space.
  • Pepper demonstrates that captivity affects animals emotionally and socially, and not just physically

Dale Jamieson — Zoos Revisited

  • Jamieson believes we should be cautious about keeping any animals in captivity.
  • He argues that zoos still rely on entertainment, even when they talk about conservation
  • The captivity takes away the freedom of animals and the ability to control their own lives

What This Site Has To Offer

  • A timeline displaying the evolution of zoos.
  • Case studies of elephants, gorillas, and other wildlife species, which will evaluate ethical concerns.
  • Applying philosophers to real-world examples.
