Zoos, Ethics, and The Evolution of Captivity

By: Huzaifa Anis
A philosophical case study exploring animal captivity, conservation, and moral responsibilities throughout history.

Project Overview

This website will serve as an interactive case study exploring how zoos have evolved throughout history, displaying a timeline dating from early civilization to modern institutions. We will highlight historic events, conservation missions, and ethical concerns regarding the Captivity and treatment of animals.

Have modern zoos transformed into ethical conservation spaces for wildlife, or do their historical pattern still indicate ethical issues that remain unresolved today in our society?

Philosophical Framework

Peter Singer — Utilitarianism & Speciesism

  • Animals can suffer; their suffering matters morally.
  • Captivity is only justified if it reduces overall suffering.
  • Raises questions about whether conservation goals outweigh the harms of captivity.

Tom Regan — Animal Rights & Inherent Value

  • Animals are “subjects-of-a-life” with rights and inherent moral worth.
  • Captivity violates autonomy and restricts natural behavior.
  • Suggests many modern zoo practices remain ethically problematic.

What You Will Find on This Site

  • A timeline displaying the evolution of zoos.
  • Case studies comparing problematic zoo practices and modern reforms.
  • Philosophical analysis applying Singer and Regan to real-world examples.
