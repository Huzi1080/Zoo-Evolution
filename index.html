Zoos, Ethics, and The Evolution of Captivity

By: Huzaifa Anis
A philosophical case study exploring animal captivity, conservation, and moral responsibilities throughout history.

Project Overview

This website will serve as an interactive case study exploring how zoos have evolved throughout history, displaying a timeline dating from early civilization to modern institutions. We will highlight historic events, conservation missions, and ethical concerns regarding the Captivity and treatment of animals.

Have modern zoos transformed into ethical conservation spaces for wildlife, or do their historical pattern indicate ethical concerns that remain unresolved in our society today?

Philosophical Framework

Peter Singer — All Animals Are Equal

Singer argues that the capacity to suffer—not intelligence or species membership—is what gives a being moral importance. Zoos must therefore justify captivity by proving that the suffering and restriction they impose are outweighed by genuine welfare or conservation benefits. For many species, this justification fails.

Tom Regan — The Case for Animal Rights

Regan claims animals have inherent value and moral rights, including the right not to be treated as objects or property. Captivity violates this right because it denies animals control, freedom, and self-determination. Even improved enclosures still treat animals as exhibits, not individuals with rights.

Pepper — Privacy Rights & Animal Agency

Pepper emphasizes that animals care about privacy, choice, and control over their social exposure. Traditional zoos deny animals the ability to withdraw from being viewed, which indicates that captivity affects more than physical health—it affects psychological dignity.

Dale Jamieson — Zoos Revisited

He argues that zoos still rely on entertainment, even when they talk about conservation. Captivity restricts agency and freedom in ways that cannot be fully justified. He concludes that the moral presumption should be against keeping animals in zoos at all unless necessary.

What This Site Has To Offer

  • A timeline displaying the evolution of zoos.
  • Case studies of elephants, gorillas, and other wildlife species, which will evaluate ethical concerns.
  • Applying philosophers to real-world examples.
